Greetings from
the World's Great Capitals

A traveller's companion to ten extraordinary cities

01 Japan

Tokyo

Japan  ·  East Asia  ·  Pop. 13.96 million

A metropolis of magnificent contradictions — ancient shrines nested between gleaming skyscrapers, whisper-quiet tea houses steps from the electric chaos of Shibuya Crossing. Tokyo rewards those who look closely. Behind every vending machine-lined alley lies a Michelin-starred ramen counter; behind every corporate tower, a pocket garden centuries old.

~ ~ ~

The city's food culture alone justifies the journey — Tokyo holds more Michelin stars than any other city on earth. Add to that the cherry blossom spectacle of Ueno Park, the neon dreamscape of Akihabara, and the serene grandeur of the Meiji Shrine, and you begin to understand why visitors return, again and again.

Points of Interest

  • Senso-ji Temple
  • Shibuya Crossing
  • Tsukiji Market
  • Shinjuku
  • Meiji Shrine
02 France

Paris

France  ·  Western Europe  ·  Pop. 2.16 million

No city on earth carries the weight of romance quite like Paris. The iron lattice of the Eiffel Tower, the endless corridors of the Louvre, the amber glow of candlelit bistros on the Left Bank — Paris is a city that has been written about for centuries and still refuses to be fully captured in words.

~ ~ ~

Walk the banks of the Seine at dusk. Lose two hours in Sainte-Chapelle. Order a croque-monsieur at a zinc bar and watch the city move around you. The Marais buzzes with galleries and design studios; Montmartre still holds the ghost of Toulouse-Lautrec. Paris rewards the wanderer above all others.

Points of Interest

  • Eiffel Tower
  • The Louvre
  • Montmartre
  • Seine River
  • Le Marais
03 Egypt

Cairo

Egypt  ·  North Africa  ·  Pop. 21.3 million

Cairo is ancient and overwhelming and completely alive. To stand at the foot of the Great Pyramid of Giza is to confront something that refuses to make sense — a structure so vast and so old that the mind simply yields. The last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World sits on the edge of a modern megacity, and the collision is extraordinary.

~ ~ ~

Beyond the pyramids, the city unfolds in layers: the medieval labyrinth of Islamic Cairo, the bazaars of Khan el-Khalili thick with spice and sound, the Egyptian Museum housing Tutankhamun's golden death mask. The Nile — eternal and slow — ties it all together.

Points of Interest

  • Pyramids of Giza
  • Egyptian Museum
  • Khan el-Khalili
  • The Nile
  • Islamic Cairo
04 Argentina

Buenos Aires

Argentina  ·  South America  ·  Pop. 3.07 million

They call it the Paris of South America, but Buenos Aires is entirely its own creature — too passionate, too loud, too alive to sit comfortably in anyone else's shadow. Steakhouses that open at midnight. Tango danced in cobblestone courtyards in La Boca. Grand Haussmann-style boulevards shaded by jacaranda trees turning purple each November.

~ ~ ~

Palermo's design boutiques and craft cocktail bars. Recoleta Cemetery, where Eva Perón rests among marble mausoleums of breathtaking grandeur. San Telmo's antique markets spilling onto Sunday streets. This is a city that takes its pleasures seriously — and invites you to do the same.

Points of Interest

  • La Boca
  • Recoleta Cemetery
  • San Telmo Market
  • Palermo
  • Teatro Colón
05 Kenya

Nairobi

Kenya  ·  East Africa  ·  Pop. 4.4 million

Nairobi is the only capital city on earth where you can watch lions roam against a skyline of glass towers. Nairobi National Park borders the city's southern edge — and at dawn, as the city begins to stir, giraffes move in silhouette across the savanna with office blocks rising behind them. It is one of the great visual spectacles in travel.

~ ~ ~

Beyond the wildlife, the city pulses with creative energy. The Westlands neighbourhood hums with restaurants, galleries, and music. The Karen Blixen Museum recalls the colonial past with quiet elegance. And Nairobi's café culture — fuelled by some of the world's finest single-origin coffee — rivals any in the world.

Points of Interest

  • Nairobi National Park
  • Giraffe Centre
  • Karen Blixen Museum
  • Westlands
  • Maasai Market
06 Thailand

Bangkok

Thailand  ·  Southeast Asia  ·  Pop. 10.5 million

Bangkok assaults the senses in the best possible way. Step out of the airport and you are immediately inside something vast and chaotic and thrilling — the smell of jasmine garlands and grilled satay, the roar of tuk-tuks threading through impossible traffic, the sudden appearance of a golden temple spire above a tangled street market.

~ ~ ~

The Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew remain among the most visually staggering complexes in the world. The Chao Phraya river ferries you past temple after temple as the city glitters on both banks. And the food — from Michelin-starred establishments to 40-baht noodle carts — is among the finest on the planet.

Points of Interest

  • Grand Palace
  • Wat Pho
  • Chatuchak Market
  • Chao Phraya
  • Khao San Road
07 Mexico

Mexico City

Mexico  ·  North America  ·  Pop. 9.2 million

Mexico City is built on a lake, on the ruins of an empire, in a valley surrounded by volcanoes. It is one of the world's great cities — vast, layered, endlessly generous to those who explore it. The Zócalo, the second-largest public square on earth, is flanked by a 700-year-old cathedral and the ruins of the Aztec Templo Mayor.

~ ~ ~

The Diego Rivera murals at the Palacio Nacional are unmissable. The Frida Kahlo Museum in Coyoacán draws pilgrims from around the world. And the taco scene — al pastor, barbacoa, carnitas — will recalibrate your understanding of what a taco can be, permanently.

Points of Interest

  • Templo Mayor
  • Frida Kahlo Museum
  • Zócalo
  • Xochimilco
  • Polanco
08 Iceland

Reykjavik

Iceland  ·  Northern Europe  ·  Pop. 128,000

The world's northernmost capital is small, strange, and utterly spectacular. Reykjavik is a city that sits at the edge of the world, where the North Atlantic crashes against volcanic shores and the sky never fully darkens in summer. The Northern Lights perform above corrugated-iron rooftops painted in candy colours.

~ ~ ~

Day trips deliver the impossible: geysers erupting every few minutes, waterfalls thundering from glacier edges, hot springs warm enough to swim in mid-winter. Back in the city, Hallgrímskirkja church towers over a scene of independent bookshops, geothermal-heated pools, and the world's most extraordinary fish soup.

Points of Interest

  • Northern Lights
  • Hallgrímskirkja
  • Blue Lagoon
  • Golden Circle
  • Harpa Concert Hall
09 New Zealand

Wellington

New Zealand  ·  Oceania  ·  Pop. 215,000

Wellington has a reputation that far exceeds its modest size. Perched dramatically on a natural harbour at the southern tip of the North Island, Wellington combines extraordinary natural beauty with the cultural density of a city three times its size. They call it "the coolest little capital in the world" — and the phrase holds up.

~ ~ ~

Te Papa Tongarewa, the national museum, is world-class. The Wellington coffee scene is fierce, obsessive, and produces exceptional espresso. And the Weta Workshop — where Middle-earth was brought to life — offers one of cinema's great behind-the-scenes experiences.

Points of Interest

  • Te Papa Museum
  • Weta Workshop
  • Mount Victoria
  • Cuba Street
  • Wellington Harbour
10 Greece

Athens

Greece  ·  Mediterranean Europe  ·  Pop. 3.15 million

Athens carries the weight of Western civilisation with remarkable ease. The Acropolis — that white marble crown on the hill above the city — has been watching over Athens for 2,500 years. Standing before the Parthenon as the Mediterranean light shifts and the city sprawls below, you feel the full improbability of human achievement.

~ ~ ~

Below the sacred rock, the Monastiraki district blends ancient ruins with flea market bustle and excellent souvlaki. The National Archaeological Museum houses artefacts that would be headliners in any collection on earth. And as the sun goes down over Lycabettus Hill, the city below turns golden.

Points of Interest

  • The Acropolis
  • Parthenon
  • National Museum
  • Monastiraki
  • Plaka District
