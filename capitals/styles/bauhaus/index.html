Ten Cities — Ten Destinations — One World

01

Tokyo

Japan — East Asia

East AsiaPop. 13.96 Million

A metropolis of magnificent contradictions — ancient shrines nested between gleaming skyscrapers, whisper-quiet tea houses steps from the electric chaos of Shibuya Crossing. Tokyo rewards those who look closely.

The city's food culture alone justifies the journey — Tokyo holds more Michelin stars than any other city on earth. Add to that the cherry blossom spectacle of Ueno Park and the neon dreamscape of Akihabara.

Senso-ji Shibuya Tsukiji Shinjuku Meiji Shrine
02

Paris

France — Western Europe

Western EuropePop. 2.16 Million

No city on earth carries the weight of romance quite like Paris. The iron lattice of the Eiffel Tower, the endless corridors of the Louvre, the amber glow of candlelit bistros on the Left Bank.

Walk the banks of the Seine at dusk. Lose two hours in Sainte-Chapelle. Order a croque-monsieur at a zinc bar and watch the city move around you. Montmartre still holds the ghost of Toulouse-Lautrec.

Eiffel Tower The Louvre Montmartre Seine Le Marais
03

Cairo

Egypt — North Africa

North AfricaPop. 21.3 Million

Cairo is ancient and overwhelming and completely alive. To stand at the foot of the Great Pyramid of Giza is to confront something that refuses to make sense — the last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World on the edge of a modern megacity.

Beyond the pyramids, the city unfolds in layers: the medieval labyrinth of Islamic Cairo, the bazaars of Khan el-Khalili thick with spice and sound, the Egyptian Museum housing Tutankhamun's golden death mask.

Pyramids Egyptian Museum Khan el-Khalili The Nile Islamic Cairo
04

Buenos Aires

Argentina — S. America

South AmericaPop. 3.07 Million

They call it the Paris of South America, but Buenos Aires is entirely its own creature — too passionate, too loud, too alive to sit comfortably in anyone else's shadow. Steakhouses that open at midnight. Tango danced in cobblestone courtyards.

Recoleta Cemetery, where Eva Perón rests among marble mausoleums of breathtaking grandeur. San Telmo's antique markets. This is a city that takes its pleasures seriously — and invites you to do the same.

La Boca Recoleta San Telmo Palermo Teatro Colón
05

Nairobi

Kenya — East Africa

East AfricaPop. 4.4 Million

Nairobi is the only capital city on earth where you can watch lions roam against a skyline of glass towers. Nairobi National Park borders the city's southern edge — at dawn, giraffes move in silhouette across the savanna with office blocks rising behind them.

Beyond the wildlife, the city pulses with creative energy. The Westlands neighbourhood hums with restaurants, galleries, and music. Nairobi's café culture — fuelled by world-class single-origin coffee — rivals any on earth.

National Park Giraffe Centre Karen Blixen Westlands Maasai Market
06

Bangkok

Thailand — SE Asia

Southeast AsiaPop. 10.5 Million

Bangkok assaults the senses in the best possible way. The smell of jasmine garlands and grilled satay, the roar of tuk-tuks threading through impossible traffic, the sudden appearance of a golden temple spire above a tangled street market.

The Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew remain among the most visually staggering complexes in the world. The food — from Michelin-starred establishments to 40-baht noodle carts — is among the finest on the planet.

Grand Palace Wat Pho Chatuchak Chao Phraya Khao San
07

Mexico City

Mexico — N. America

North AmericaPop. 9.2 Million

Mexico City is built on a lake, on the ruins of an empire, in a valley surrounded by volcanoes. The Zócalo is flanked by a 700-year-old cathedral and the ruins of the Aztec Templo Mayor, sitting in impossible proximity to each other.

The Diego Rivera murals at the Palacio Nacional are unmissable. The Frida Kahlo Museum draws pilgrims from around the world. And the taco scene will recalibrate your understanding of what food can be, permanently.

Templo Mayor Frida Kahlo Zócalo Xochimilco Polanco
08

Reykjavik

Iceland — N. Europe

Northern EuropePop. 128,000

The world's northernmost capital is small, strange, and utterly spectacular. Reykjavik sits at the edge of the world — the sky never fully darkens in summer, and in winter, the Northern Lights perform above corrugated-iron rooftops painted in candy colours.

Day trips deliver the impossible: geysers erupting every few minutes, waterfalls thundering from glacier edges, hot springs warm enough to swim in mid-winter. Back in the city, Hallgrímskirkja towers above everything.

Northern Lights Hallgrímskirkja Blue Lagoon Golden Circle Harpa
09

Wellington

New Zealand — Oceania

OceaniaPop. 215,000

Wellington has a reputation that far exceeds its modest size. Perched dramatically on a natural harbour, Wellington combines extraordinary natural beauty with the cultural density of a city three times its size. Te Papa Tongarewa is genuinely world-class.

The Weta Workshop — where Middle-earth was brought to life — offers one of cinema's great behind-the-scenes experiences. The Wellington coffee scene is fierce, obsessive, and exceptional. The wind is also exceptional.

Te Papa Weta Workshop Mt Victoria Cuba Street Harbour
10

Athens

Greece — Mediterranean

Mediterranean EuropePop. 3.15 Million

Athens carries the weight of Western civilisation with remarkable ease. The Acropolis — that white marble crown on the hill above the city — has been watching over Athens for 2,500 years. Standing before the Parthenon, you feel the full improbability of human achievement.

Below the sacred rock, the Monastiraki district blends ancient ruins with flea market bustle and excellent souvlaki. As the sun goes down over Lycabettus Hill, the city below turns golden — one of the world's great spectacles.

Acropolis Parthenon National Museum Monastiraki Plaka
