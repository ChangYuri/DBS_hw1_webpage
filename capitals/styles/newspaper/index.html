The Traveller's Broadsheet — Ten Capital Cities of the World

The World Capitals

"To travel is to discover that everyone is wrong about other countries."

Tokyo

Japan · East Asia · Pop. 13.96M

Ancient shrines, electric streets, and more Michelin stars than any city on earth. Tokyo is the world's greatest city for those willing to get delightfully lost.

See: Senso-ji Temple, Shibuya Crossing, Meiji Shrine, Akihabara

Paris

France · Western Europe · Pop. 2.16M

The City of Light still earns every superlative. From the Louvre's miles of masterworks to candlelit bistros on the Left Bank — Paris is simply, enduringly magnificent.

See: Eiffel Tower, The Louvre, Montmartre, Le Marais

Cairo

Egypt · North Africa · Pop. 21.3M

Stand before the Pyramids of Giza — the last of the Seven Wonders still standing — and feel five thousand years of history press against you. Utterly humbling.

See: Pyramids of Giza, Egyptian Museum, Khan el-Khalili, The Nile

Buenos Aires

Argentina · South America · Pop. 3.07M

This city is pure passion — tango in cobblestone alleys, steak served at midnight, grand boulevards alive with jacaranda. Buenos Aires demands you live without compromise.

"Life is too short for a bad steak or a slow tango."

See: La Boca, Recoleta Cemetery, San Telmo, Teatro Colón

Nairobi

Kenya · East Africa · Pop. 4.4M

Lions in the morning, world-class coffee by noon — Nairobi is Africa's most dynamic capital and the only city on earth with a national park on its doorstep.

See: Nairobi National Park, Giraffe Centre, Karen Blixen Museum

Bangkok

Thailand · Southeast Asia · Pop. 10.5M

Golden temples, extraordinary street food, and a pulse that never drops. Bangkok is sensory overload in the best possible way — chaotic, glorious, and completely unforgettable.

See: Grand Palace, Wat Pho, Chatuchak Market, Chao Phraya

Mexico City

Mexico · North America · Pop. 9.2M

Built on an Aztec empire in a valley of volcanoes. The murals are magnificent, the tacos are life-changing, and the history runs deeper than you can fathom in a single visit.

See: Templo Mayor, Frida Kahlo Museum, Zócalo, Xochimilco

Reykjavik

Iceland · Northern Europe · Pop. 128,000

Northern Lights overhead. Geysers erupting across volcanic plains. Hot springs in mid-winter. Reykjavik is the world's northernmost capital and one of its most extraordinary.

"The sky here does things you won't find anywhere else."

See: Northern Lights, Hallgrímskirkja, Blue Lagoon, Golden Circle

Wellington

New Zealand · Oceania · Pop. 215,000

The coolest small capital on earth — world-class museum, ferocious coffee culture, and the Weta Workshop where Middle-earth was built. Wellington punches well above its weight.

See: Te Papa Museum, Weta Workshop, Mount Victoria, Cuba Street

Athens

Greece · Mediterranean · Pop. 3.15M

Where democracy, philosophy, and drama were born. The Acropolis watches over the city as it has for 2,500 years — and below, Athens buzzes with life, souvlaki, and summer light.

See: The Acropolis, Parthenon, National Museum, Monastiraki, Plaka
