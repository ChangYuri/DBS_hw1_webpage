I have a competition in me

World CapitalsTen Cities. No Milkshake.

I am a traveller of the old school. I go to a place alone and I do not leave until I have drunk every last thing it has to offer.

No. 1

Tokyo

Japan · East Asia

I've travelled this earth. I've looked for promising cities. And I tell you, gentlemen — there is nothing in this world quite like standing in a Tokyo street at two in the morning with a bowl of ramen in your hands and a city of fourteen million people moving around you like a very polite river. That is something.

Population

13.96M

Senso-ji Temple Shibuya Crossing Tsukiji Market Meiji Shrine
No. 2

Paris

France · Western Europe

I came to Paris to see what the fuss was about. I found beauty everywhere I looked and not a single apology for it. The Louvre alone contains more ambition than most countries have produced in their entire history. I will not say I liked it. I will say I understood it, which is more important.

Population

2.16M

Eiffel Tower The Louvre Montmartre Le Marais
No. 3

Cairo

Egypt · North Africa

The Pyramids have been sitting there for four thousand five hundred years. That is not ambition. That is something else entirely. Something I understand in my bones. You build something and then you look at it and you think — yes. That will last. Cairo built the only things on earth that actually will.

Population

21.3M

Pyramids of Giza Egyptian Museum Khan el-Khalili The Nile
No. 4

Buenos Aires

Argentina · South America

There is a competition in this city. You feel it immediately. Every restaurant is competing with every other restaurant. Every tango dancer is watching the others. And the steak — I have tried the steak in seventeen countries — this steak wins. I have a competition in me. Buenos Aires satisfies it.

Population

3.07M

La Boca Recoleta Cemetery San Telmo Teatro Colón
No. 5

Nairobi

Kenya · East Africa

I came to Nairobi without any associates. I came alone, as I prefer it. At dawn the next morning I stood at the edge of the national park and watched lions cross the plain. Behind me: a skyline. Ahead of me: the wild. I have drilled in thirty states and eight countries. I have never seen anything like this.

Population

4.4M

National Park Giraffe Centre Karen Blixen Westlands
No. 6

Bangkok

Thailand · Southeast Asia

The gold here is real. That is the first thing you notice. Four hundred temples, and every one of them covered in something that cost something. Bangkok is a city that decided to show its wealth as openly as possible, and found that beauty was the result. I respect that kind of decision.

Population

10.5M

Grand Palace Wat Pho Chatuchak Chao Phraya
No. 7

Mexico City

Mexico · North America

There is something in the soil here. Literally. They built this city on a lake, on the rubble of an empire, and still it rises. The Zócalo alone — second largest public square on earth — tells you everything about a people who do not think small. I do not think small either. We understand one another.

Population

9.2M

Templo Mayor Frida Kahlo Zócalo Xochimilco
No. 8

Reykjavik

Iceland · Northern Europe

I have seen a lot of barren land. I know what it looks like and I know what it is worth. Iceland is volcanic — raw material, all of it, right there under your feet. And then the Northern Lights come out, and you understand that this is not barren land. This is the most dramatic land on earth. It simply keeps its drama for the night.

Population

128K

Northern Lights Hallgrímskirkja Blue Lagoon Golden Circle
No. 9

Wellington

New Zealand · Oceania

Small doesn't mean anything. I've come up out of nothing. Wellington has come up out of the bottom of the earth, at the end of the earth, and made itself into something worth everything. The museum alone. The coffee alone. If I had a son I would send him to Wellington to learn what serious work looks like.

Population

215K

Te Papa Museum Weta Workshop Mt Victoria Cuba Street
No. 10

Athens

Greece · Mediterranean

I'm finished. That is what Athens makes you feel. You climb the Acropolis, you stand in front of the Parthenon, and you think — I am finished. Not defeated. Completed. Everything built after this was built in response to this. This is where the well was first drilled. And it has never run dry.

Population

3.15M

The Acropolis Parthenon National Museum Monastiraki
